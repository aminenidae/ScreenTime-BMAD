# Appfigures ASO Strategy & Insights 🚀

Based on the analysis of the most recent and highly relevant videos from the [Appfigures YouTube Channel](https://www.youtube.com/@appfigures), here are the most critical strategies to optimize your App Store rankings for **Brain Coinz** (now that the app is officially distributed).

## 1. Massive Algorithm Update: Screenshots Now Impact Ranking 🚨
Apple made a major unannounced change recently: **The algorithm now uses AI to read text from your App Store screenshots** and factors those keywords into your search rank.

> [!WARNING] 
> If you don't have text on your screenshots or the text is illegible to OCR, you are losing out on a major ranking signal.

**Actionable Tips for Screenshots:**
- **Replace Passive Text with Active Keywords:** Change fuzzy captions (e.g., "Easy to use") into high-intent keywords someone would search for (e.g., "Manage Kids Screen Time").
- **Contrast is King:** Ensure there is high contrast between the text and background (e.g., dark text on light background or vice versa).
- **Legibility:** Avoid heavily stylized, thin, or compressed fonts. Stay away from heavy neon glows or fuzzy, blurred shadows which scramble OCR.
- **Duplicate Your Main Keywords:** *Contrary to metadata rules*, doing this in screenshots actually helps. If your most important keyword is in your Title, put it in your screenshots too to give it extra weight!
- **Keep it Focused:** Do not stuff too many keywords into a single screenshot. Stick to high-impact keywords.

## 2. Leverage Custom Product Pages (CPPs) for High Conversion 📈
Custom Product Pages let you create alternate versions of your app store page with different screenshots and promotional text. Since you pay for impressions (or work hard organically), CPPs dramatically increase your conversion rates by matching the user's intent perfectly. 

> [!TIP]
> CPPs aren't for A/B testing colors; they are for segmenting intents. If a user searches for "math timer," the CPP should show screenshots of the math tasks. If they search for "chore rewards," the CPP should show the chores interface.

**CPP Strategy:**
- Use your **Default Product Page** to target your primary, highest-volume keyword.
- Create **Custom Product Pages** to target *Long-tail Keywords*. When a user discovers the app through a specific niche, the screenshots should match that exact niche.
- Keep branding consistent across CPPs, just swap the features highlighted to match the search query.

## 3. Finding and Targeting the Right Keywords 🔍
The biggest mistake is aiming too high on competitive keywords (e.g., "Screen Time") from day one. You need to focus on **long-tail keyword opportunities**.

- **Keyword Hierarchy:** The algorithm prioritizes left-to-right. 
  1. App Name (Most Important)
  2. Subtitle 
  3. Keyword List (Least Important)
- **Finding the "Sweet Spot":** Look for keywords with a **Popularity between 20-60** where the top 5 ranking apps *do not* have the target keyword properly included in their App Name. This is your "in."
- **Keep it Focused:** The algorithm splits 100% of its attention across the characters you provide. If you just stuff keywords randomly, each keyword gets diluted. Use only the most relevant keywords.

## 4. The "10x Keyword List" Localization Trick 🌎
This is an incredibly powerful ASO "hack" specific to the US App Store.

Apple indexes keywords from **10 different language localizations** (e.g., Spanish, Russian, Chinese, French, etc.) to serve diverse communities in the US. However, Apple *does not check the language of the keywords you provide.*

> [!IMPORTANT]
> You can place **English keywords into foreign localizations** in App Store Connect. Apple will index them in the US App Store as if they were in your primary English metadata.

**How to Execute:**
- Instead of the standard 160-character limit, you gain 10x the space. 
- Expand your long-tail keyword reach by inserting permutations of "Screen time", "digital allowance", "screen limits", etc., into the keyword lists of the secondary localizations.
- **Rule of Thumb:** Apple *does not merge* words across different localizations. So, if you want to rank for "Screen Limits", both "Screen" and "Limits" must be tightly coupled within the *same* localization keyword list.
