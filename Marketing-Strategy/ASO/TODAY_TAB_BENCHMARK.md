_Last updated: 2026-04-21_

## 🔴 FIELD FINDING (user capture 2026-04-21) — reframes the pitch

Direct Today-tab inspection:

1. **"App of the Day" exists but is a single card per day.** It is the only daily non-game app slot in the entire Today tab. Everything else is a game feature (Game of the Day, game collections, game hero stories).
2. **~99% of Today-tab inventory is games.** Non-game editorial real estate in Today is effectively just that one App of the Day card, plus occasional app-only stories.

**Implications for pitch strategy:**
- App of the Day IS a valid pitch target — but it's a **one-slot-per-day, 365 slots/year** surface. Competition per slot is high. The realistic cadence for an indie in our niche is one shot every few months, not routine placement.
- Far more reliable editorial targets:
  - **Curated collection membership** (apps.apple.com/us/story/id... — the evergreen "Apps for..." lists)
  - **Apps-tab editorial stories** (separate surface from Today)
  - **Editors' Choice badge** on the app's listing
- Best pitch structure: lead ask = collection/Editors' Choice, stretch ask = App of the Day tied to a specific moment (Back-to-School week, Family Safety month, iOS launch story).
- The web-research patterns below still hold for **how** featured apps are positioned (indie story, screenshot style, pricing, review volume) — only the **pitched surface** changes.

## Purpose

This document benchmarks how Apple's App Store editorial team has surfaced parenting / screen-time / family / kids-education apps in the recent past (primarily 2024 App Store Awards cycle through Q2 2026), so that Brain Coinz can position its editorial pitch against proven patterns rather than generic "please feature us" copy. Brain Coinz is a 0-download, 0-review indie family screen-time rewards app (kids earn screen time by hitting learning goals). Version 1.0.4 ships 2026-05-01. The editorial angles we are testing are: (a) novel use of the Family Controls / DeviceActivity framework, (b) indie-developer story, (c) Back-to-School positive-behavior framing, (d) a true rewards model vs pure blocking, which distinguishes us from Opal/Jomo/one sec.

## Methodology

Evidence was gathered via WebSearch + WebFetch across Apple Newsroom, Apple Developer, MacStories, 9to5Mac, TechCrunch, TUAW, Tom's Guide, developer blogs, and the apps' current App Store listings (apps.apple.com). Every deep-dive cross-references the live App Store page to capture editor quotes, "Editors' Choice" badges, curated-list memberships, and pricing. I cannot browse the live iOS Today tab, so current-day Today-tab placement and date-specific "App of the Day" slotting must be supplemented by user-captured screenshots (see Gaps section). Anything not verifiable from a credible source is marked "suspected." Dates use YYYY-MM-DD.

## Summary Findings

- **"Editors' Choice" is the most common editorial signal you can confirm from the public App Store page**; explicit "App of the Day YYYY-MM-DD" attribution is almost never exposed in page metadata and must be captured from the Today tab in real time or from developer/press announcements.
- **Curated-list membership is the most durable editorial asset.** Nearly every featured screen-time / wellbeing app we verified (Opal, Endel, Jomo, one sec, Finch) carries membership in at least one evergreen collection ("Manage ADHD With These Helpful Apps", "Make Way for Your Mental Health", "The Best Accessible Apps and Games", "Fitness and Wellness Apps by Women"). Pitches should name the collection you want to belong to, not just ask for a day.
- **Indie, solo-ish teams win editorial love when they have a distinct narrative hook.** Crouton (Devin Davies, Christchurch NZ), Pok Pok (mom-founded, hand-drawn), CapWords (language learning via camera), one sec (Frederik Riedel, self-built for his own social-media problem), and Jomo (Women's History Month collection) all got attention from a single-sentence story.
- **Review volume is NOT a prerequisite for first feature.** Confirmed feature winners range from Jomo (~1.9K ratings) and Crouton (~2.6K) up to Finch (683K) and Bluey (1M). Low review count appears to be disqualifying only when paired with no distinctive positioning. Brain Coinz at 0 reviews is below the low end we could find, which is a real risk; building to ~500 reviews before the August pitch is a reasonable target.
- **Screenshot style splits into two camps by niche.** Kids/education apps (Khan Academy Kids, Sago Mini, Bluey, Pok Pok, Lingokids, Duolingo ABC) use illustrated-character, low-UI, lifestyle-of-play visuals. Screen-time / wellbeing apps (Opal, Jomo, one sec, Endel, Stoic, Finch) use UI-centric hero screens with dashboards, analytics, and bold text overlays. Brain Coinz sits at the intersection — the closest visual precedent is Bluey/Sago Mini (child experience) crossed with Opal/Finch (parent dashboard).
- **Free-with-IAP + subscription is the dominant pricing model for featured wellbeing apps** (Opal $4.99–99.99, Jomo $5.99–99.99, Finch $5.99–69.99, Endel $2.99–119.99, one sec $19.99/yr + $99.99 lifetime, Stoic $6.99–99.99). Lifetime pricing is notably common; Apple editorial does not appear to penalize aggressive pricing so long as the free tier is usable.
- **Back-to-School and mental-health-awareness windows are the two most reliable editorial moments for our niche.** Confirmed BtS placements in 2024–2025 are lighter than expected at the App Store level; Apple's 2025 BtS push was primarily hardware-accessory promotion, not an editorial push, which suggests the editorial BtS story slot is underused and pitchable.
- **Apple is actively reshaping the Today tab around kid-safety in 2025–2026.** iOS 26 (June 2025) added age-rating filtering that hides apps above a parent's age cap from Today, Games, and Apps tabs. A screen-time-rewards app that positions as a complement to Apple's own parental controls (rather than a workaround) aligns with Apple's current messaging.

## Patterns by Dimension

### Screenshot styles that get featured
- **Kids category:** Illustrated characters dominate; UI chrome is minimized or absent. Mascot presence is common (Kodi/Khan Academy, Finch/Finch, Sago Mini animals, Duolingo owl, Bluey cast, Pok Pok's hand-drawn world).
- **Screen-time / wellbeing category:** Bold UI hero shots. Numerical dashboards, streaks, analytics charts, and big typographic claims ("saved 1h 23m/day"). Text overlays are heavy.
- **Implication for Brain Coinz:** We straddle both. The winning approach is likely a 2-persona screenshot deck — first 3 screens parent-dashboard UI (Opal/Finch style), screens 4–6 child illustrated mascot / reward moment (Pok Pok / Bluey style). This matches the "Locked Plan" already in `project_1_0_4_screenshot_set.md`.

### Pricing models that get featured
- **Free + IAP subscription** is the default (Opal, Jomo, Finch, Stoic, Endel, one sec, Sago Mini, Lingokids, Bluey, Pok Pok).
- **100% free** is strong signal (Khan Academy Kids, Duolingo ABC).
- **Lifetime pricing** tier ($79–$125) is common and not penalized.
- **No featured app in the niche is paid-upfront.** This is an important disqualifier.

### Developer size / story
- **Solo / tiny team wins with a story:** Crouton (Devin Davies, NZ solo), one sec (Frederik Riedel, Germany solo), Pok Pok (mom-founder), Jomo (woman-founder), Widgetsmith (David Smith, solo).
- **Small-mid indie studios** are the editorial sweet spot for multi-year coverage (Pok Pok, Opal, Endel, Finch).
- **Big publishers** get Award finalist slots but rarely "Apps We Love" indie editorial love (Bluey/Budge, Duolingo).
- **Nonprofits** get permanent collection status (Khan Academy Kids).

### Common editorial angles (confirmed from editor quotes and collection names)
- "Manage ADHD With These Helpful Apps" — repeatedly surfaced for screen-time / focus apps
- "Make Way for Your Mental Health" — wellbeing collection
- "The Best Accessible Apps and Games" — accessibility crossover
- "Fitness and Wellness Apps by Women" — Women's History Month (March)
- "Spark Your Child's Imagination" — evergreen kids collection
- "See What's New in iOS N" — for apps that ship day-one support for OS features
- "Family Connection" framing (NYT Games 2024 Award write-up)
- Apple Design Awards categories directly relevant to us: **Interaction** (Crouton won 2024), **Inclusivity** (Speechify won 2025), **Delight and Fun** (CapWords won 2025)

## Gaps / questions that user screenshots will answer

Web research cannot tell us any of the following — these are what the user-captured Today tab screenshots should deliberately document:

1. **What is on the Today tab right now (April 2026)?** Is there an active kids/family/learning story? Is any screen-time app currently in "App of the Day" rotation?
2. **How is the Today tab structured post iOS 26 age-filter changes?** Does "family" content appear differently for Child Accounts vs adult accounts?
3. **What is the current cadence of kids/family editorial stories?** Are they weekly? Monthly? Tied only to BtS and holidays?
4. **What curated collection pages does Apple currently host for our niche?** Collection URLs (apps.apple.com/us/story/id...) need capturing; these are pitch targets by name.
5. **How many screenshots does a featured Today card use?** Specifically: 1 hero image? Carousel? Video? This determines what creative asset the pitch must lead with.
6. **Does any 2026 Today story feature a screen-time-rewards model specifically?** If not, that is an empty quadrant Brain Coinz can pitch into.
7. **What is Apple editorial tone right now — playful, utilitarian, wellbeing-forward?** Tone drift matters for the pitch voice.
8. **Which competitors have a Developer Story page on the App Store?** (apps.apple.com/us/developer-story/...) These are the strongest proof points we can aim for; need to audit which competitors have them.
