# 1.0.4 Pre-Submission Keyword Intent Audit

**Date:** 2026-04-27
**Tool:** Astro `mcp__astro__search_app_store`, top 20 results, US store
**Audit scope:** US-direct token pool only (Name + Subtitle + EN_US KW). ES_MX-only tokens excluded per user decision.

## Executive Summary

**The 1.0.4 keyword strategy has a systemic intent-mismatch problem that is NOT solved by the Name change to "Brain Coinz: Parental Control".**

Of the **30 keywords audited** across the 1.0.4 indexable surface:

- ✅ **FIT (parent target):** **3** — `parental` (single), `parenting` (single), `positive parenting` (2-word). Plus `parental control` and `parental control app` (already verified in prior session).
- 🟡 **MIXED:** **4** — `educational` (single), `positive reinforcement` (Tier C), `motivate kids` (peripheral), `educational play` (kid-edu lean — best 2-word find). `positive reinforcement app` also mixed.
- ❌ **LEAK (off-target):** **23** — including the ENTIRE `limit/screen/time/reduce` cluster.

**Critical finding: every single phrase in the 1.0.4 draft's locked Tier A/B targets — the phrases the metadata was specifically designed to land — is a ❌ LEAK.** The Subtitle's primary phrase `limit screen time` (Pop 23) returns 0/20 parent-target apps. The highest-Pop Tier B target `screen time control` (Pop 50) returns 0/20 parent-target apps. All four Day-14 indexing canaries (`time limit`, `app limit`, `screen limit`, `screen time limit`) return 0/20 parent-target apps. They sit in adult digital wellness SERPs (ScreenZen, Opal, BePresent, one sec, Brick, Freedom, Jomo, Brainrot, Roots, ClearSpace, Refocus).

The Name change fixes the Name's SERP position (`parental control` → 20/20 PARENT) but does NOT propagate to the indexed tokens that drive 2-3 word search matches. Apple's tokenizer joins our Name+Subtitle+KW tokens into combinations the way the engine indexes — and most of those combinations (everything not anchored on `parental`/`parenting`) leak to wrong-audience SERPs.

**Strategic recommendation before submitting 1.0.4:** the Subtitle and the entire KW field need rethinking. Hold submission until the user-led keyword discovery for parent-intent terms is complete.

## Recommended Action Before 1.0.4 Submission

**1. The Subtitle is the biggest leak.** `Limit screen time, differently` is structurally pointing at adult digital wellness searchers. The Pop 23 phrase it targets is 1/20 parent. Even if we rank #5 organically, ~95% of impressions go to wrong-audience users who will not click. The whole Subtitle should be reconsidered with parent-intent verbs (`monitor`, `manage`, `kids', `family`).

**2. The KW field carries over too many adult-wellness tokens.** `motivate, positive, reinforcement, educational, limit, reduce, play, unlock, brainrot, lock` — 10 of 12 tokens lead our indexing toward off-target SERPs. The new additions (`parenting, control`) are good. Of the carryover tokens:
- KEEP: `educational` (mixed, edu-adjacent)
- KEEP w/caveat: `positive`, `reinforcement` (only useful when paired = `positive parenting` ✅, `positive reinforcement` 🟡)
- DROP-CANDIDATE: `limit, reduce, screen, time, lock` — all single-token leaks; even paired they leak (every Tier A phrase ❌)
- DROP-CANDIDATE: `motivate, play, unlock, brainrot` — Tier 1 leaks; their pairings rarely help (`motivate kids` mixed-leak; `unlock games` leak; `play games` leak)

**3. The Day-14 measurement gate (2026-05-01) is now misframed.** "Did the canaries index?" is the wrong question. The right question is "did `parental control`, `parental control app`, `positive parenting`, `educational play` deliver parent-target impressions?" — those are the 4 phrases out of the entire 1.0.4 indexable surface that point at our actual customers.

**4. A 1.0.4 keyword overhaul before submission is recommended.** Per the user's explicit guidance, the next step is user-led keyword discovery for parent-intent terms. The audit findings give clear data: `parental, parenting, family, kids, child, monitor, manage, block-for-kids, screentime-for-kids, parent-app, parent-mode` and similar parent-action-verbs are the missing token category. The discovery step should focus there.
