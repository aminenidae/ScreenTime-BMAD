**`Temps d'écran, devoirs, jeux`** (28/30 chars)

Rationale: `contrôle parental` search results in France are saturated by GPS-tracker and generic-blocker apps (Google Family Link, Nintendo, Qustodio, Kidslox, FamiSafe, Kids360 — none doing what Tic Lock does). But a live, low-competition niche exists: searching the natural French phrase for "screen time + homework" returns only 6 apps total, led by a direct competitor ("Mr Arthur: Temps Écran Éducatif" — "Apprendre en s'amusant") doing our exact positioning. This subtitle signals that niche in plain language without repeating "contrôle parental" from the Name.

```
Stop fighting over screen time.

Tic Lock is an automated app blocker that turns screen time into a proven "Learn-to-Play" reward system. Tic Lock controls iOS apps on your child's iPhone or iPad. The app automatically tracks the time your child spends on educational apps and instantly unlocks their entertainment apps. No manual tracking, no arguments.

**How does this app blocker work?**

A parent creates a simple rule: "30 minutes of Khan Academy unlocks 60 minutes of YouTube." That's it. Tic Lock monitors educational app usage natively. The instant the timer hits the goal, reward apps are unlocked. When earned time runs out, the apps lock again.

**Why is this different from existing parental controls?**

Unlike legacy timers that rely on punishing restrictions, Tic Lock motivates kids to *want* to learn through positive reinforcement. Unlike chore apps that require parent approval, Tic Lock is fully automated.

Bring Your Own Apps: Khan Academy, Duolingo, Prodigy — you choose what counts.
```
