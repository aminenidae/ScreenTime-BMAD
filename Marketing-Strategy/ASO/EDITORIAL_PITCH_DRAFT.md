## Pitch — primary draft (≤ 500 words, the form's typical limit)

> **Brain Coinz turns the screen-time fight into a learning loop kids actually want.**
>
> I'm an indie developer (solo, no funding). 1.0.4 is the version I want to share with you — it's the first build where the product, the App Store page, and the family-mode flow all line up the way I designed them.
>
> **The thesis.** Every screen-time app on the App Store today either blocks apps (OurPact, Bark, Qustodio) or gamifies the parent's restriction (Opal, Jomo, one sec — for adults). None of them sit on the kid's side. Brain Coinz uses Apple's Family Controls and DeviceActivity APIs to do something specific: a child's real Khan Academy / Duolingo / Prodigy time automatically unlocks their reward apps. Parents set the trade-off once. The system runs itself. Kids stop negotiating with parents and start negotiating with themselves about how much learning earns how much play.
>
> **Why this is a Family Controls story.** I built this against Family Controls *as the platform feature*. The unlock decisions happen in a DeviceActivityMonitor extension running on the child's device — there's no surveillance, no parent-side tracking of what the child is doing, no cloud upload of usage. Apple's 2025-06 expanded parental tools framing is exactly where Brain Coinz lives: the child gets agency, the parent gets a system that doesn't require them to be the bad guy. We are an example of the framework being used the way the design intent suggests.
>
> **What I'd love your help with.** Three asks, in order:
>
> 1. **Inclusion in "Manage ADHD With These Helpful Apps."** Brain Coinz's positive-reinforcement loop with visible progress + automatic external limits is a textbook executive-function support pattern. The ADHD families our beta testers sent us were the most enthusiastic cohort.
> 2. **A "Family Connection" or "Positive Parental Controls" curated collection.** If one doesn't exist yet, the category is conspicuously empty — Brain Coinz, Greenlight (allowance), and a small handful of homework-tracker apps could anchor it. We'd be honoured to be the screen-time entry.
> 3. **Editors' Choice on the listing.** Long-shot for a 0-review app, but the design and onboarding are where I spent the year. The screenshot set in the attached Custom Product Page is the proof.
>
> **Practicalities.** 1.0.4 is in review now (submitted 2026-05-01) and ships within the week. The page will be at v1.0.4 the moment editorial wants to look. If August / Back-to-School is the right placement window, that gives us 3 months of trial-cohort data and reviews before the slot — happy to share whatever telemetry helps you decide.
>
> Thank you for the time it took to read this.
>
> — Amine
> aminenidae@gmail.com

## Risks / things this pitch deliberately does NOT do

- **Does not lead with "App of the Day."** Per benchmark, the form-pitch lead-with-collection path is far higher base rate.
- **Does not name competitors as inferior.** Apple editorial reads as a cooperative voice, not a comparison ad. The "every screen-time app blocks; we reward" line is positioning, not attack copy.
- **Does not promise downloads/reviews/funding milestones.** Apple editorial doesn't reward velocity claims; they reward craft + thesis. The TODAY_TAB_BENCHMARK confirms low-review apps win when the hook is distinctive.
- **Does not request a specific date.** Asking for a date is a tell of indie inexperience; surfaces ask for windows (BtS, Family Safety, iOS launch).
- **Does not include screenshots in-line.** The CPP URL is the screenshot deck — letting Apple click through to the page they'd ultimately feature is the cleanest signal.
