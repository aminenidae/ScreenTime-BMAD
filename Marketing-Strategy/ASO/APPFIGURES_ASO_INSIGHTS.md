# Appfigures ASO Strategy & Insights 🚀

Based on the analysis of the most recent and highly relevant videos from the [Appfigures YouTube Channel](https://www.youtube.com/@appfigures), here are the most critical strategies to optimize your App Store rankings for **Brain Coinz** (now that the app is officially distributed).

## 1. Massive Algorithm Update: Screenshots Now Impact Ranking 🚨
Apple made a major unannounced change recently: **The algorithm now uses AI to read text from your App Store screenshots** and factors those keywords into your search rank.

> [!WARNING] 
> If you don't have text on your screenshots or the text is illegible to OCR, you are losing out on a major ranking signal.

**Actionable Tips for Screenshots:**
- **Replace Passive Text with Active Keywords:** Change fuzzy captions (e.g., "Easy to use") into high-intent keywords someone would search for (e.g., "Manage Kids Screen Time").
- **Contrast is King:** Ensure there is high contrast between the text and background (e.g., dark text on light background or vice versa).
- **Legibility:** Avoid heavily stylized, thin, or compressed fonts. Stay away from heavy neon glows or fuzzy, blurred shadows which scramble OCR.
- **Duplicate Your Main Keywords:** *Contrary to metadata rules*, doing this in screenshots actually helps. If your most important keyword is in your Title, put it in your screenshots too to give it extra weight!
- **Keep it Focused:** Do not stuff too many keywords into a single screenshot. Stick to high-impact keywords.

## 2. Leverage Custom Product Pages (CPPs) for High Conversion 📈
Custom Product Pages let you create alternate versions of your app store page with different screenshots and promotional text. Since you pay for impressions (or work hard organically), CPPs dramatically increase your conversion rates by matching the user's intent perfectly. 

> [!TIP]
> CPPs aren't for A/B testing colors; they are for segmenting intents. If a user searches for "math timer," the CPP should show screenshots of the math tasks. If they search for "chore rewards," the CPP should show the chores interface.

**CPP Strategy:**
- Use your **Default Product Page** to target your primary, highest-volume keyword.
- Create **Custom Product Pages** to target *Long-tail Keywords*. When a user discovers the app through a specific niche, the screenshots should match that exact niche.
- Keep branding consistent across CPPs, just swap the features highlighted to match the search query.

---

## 3. Finding and Targeting the Right Keywords 🔍
The biggest mistake is aiming too high on competitive keywords (e.g., "Screen Time") from day one. You need to focus on **long-tail keyword opportunities**.

- **Keyword Hierarchy:** The algorithm prioritizes left-to-right. 
  1. App Name (Most Important)
  2. Subtitle 
  3. Keyword List (Least Important)
- **Finding the "Sweet Spot":** Look for keywords with a **Popularity between 20-60** where the top 5 ranking apps *do not* have the target keyword properly included in their App Name. This is your "in."
- **Keep it Focused:** The algorithm splits 100% of its attention across the characters you provide. If you just stuff keywords randomly, each keyword gets diluted. Use only the most relevant keywords.

---

## 4. The "10x Keyword List" Localization Trick 🌎
This is an incredibly powerful ASO "hack" specific to the US App Store.

Apple indexes keywords from **10 different language localizations** (e.g., Spanish, Russian, Chinese, French, etc.) to serve diverse communities in the US. However, Apple *does not check the language of the keywords you provide.*

> [!IMPORTANT]
> You can place **English keywords into foreign localizations** in App Store Connect. Apple will index them in the US App Store as if they were in your primary English metadata.

**How to Execute:**
- Instead of the standard 160-character limit, you gain 10x the space. 
- Expand your long-tail keyword reach by inserting permutations of "Screen time", "digital allowance", "screen limits", etc., into the keyword lists of the secondary localizations.
- **Rule of Thumb:** Apple *does not merge* words across different localizations. So, if you want to rank for "Screen Limits", both "Screen" and "Limits" must be tightly coupled within the *same* localization keyword list.

### 5. How Apple's Algorithm Actually Treats Ratings 🌟

- **Volume matters, not stars.** Apple's algorithm uses the *number* of ratings to judge popularity, not the star value. Verbatim (`2UID7_CPCi0`): *"The actual value of the rating, whether it's one or five or four or three, is completely irrelevant. Completely."*
- **Cadence matters.** Verbatim: *"If you get them continually on and on every week over the course of about 30 days, the algorithm will learn that your app is something that it should elevate."*
- **Retention is NOT an Apple ranking factor.** Verbatim (`cCwI5BgGhdw`): *"On the Apple side of things higher number of ratings retention does not play into it."* Google Play does use retention (specifically immediate uninstall / crash signals), but this is Apple-specific.
- **Prompt for ratings EARLY** (`-8PrvZKx-VA`). Verbatim: *"75% of people, if not more, are gone by the next day. So, if you don't trigger a rating prompt early on, you never give them a chance to rate."* Early simple prompt beats sophisticated "delight-moment" timing because the delight moment often never arrives for most users.

### 6. Keyword Placement Hierarchy — Beginning of Name is Load-Bearing 🎯

- Verbatim from the Rain Alarm teardown (`qmtDVU9CLv8`): *"Rain Alarm is number seven in its category with no ratings, no new ratings in the last 30 days... because rain alarm is the first thing in the name of the app. So what you can learn from this is putting stuff in the beginning of your app's name is actually really really powerful even if you don't have ratings."*
- Complementary advice (`2UID7_CPCi0`): *"Put brand name for your app. Put it at the end. Make it very short."*
- Implication: **keyword first, brand last**. Live Brain Coinz name is "Brain Coinz: Earn Screen Time" (brand first) — this is worth revisiting after the Day 14 / Day 21 measurement window to evaluate a flip to "Earn Screen Time: Brain Coinz".

### 7. Longtail Keywords Are the Budget Hack for Small Advertisers 💡

Ariel's single most emphasized tactic across both AMA sessions — verbatim (`9gzE3rmVXMI`): *"The one hack that makes every campaign that I've ever touched better."*

- A longtail keyword is 2–3 words describing intent (e.g., `earn screen time for kids`, not `screen time`).
- Single-word keywords in competitive categories can cost $5–$10 per tap. Longtail keywords typically cost a fraction of that with higher intent.
- For Brain Coinz: `screen time`, `parental control`, `app blocker` are too expensive to auction against incumbents. But `earn screen time for kids`, `kids learning rewards`, `learn to unlock apps` are within reach.

### 8. Brand Bidding — Contested Advice ⚠️

Two conflicting positions from industry experts, both current (2025):

- **Adam Lyttle:** Run an Apple Search Ads "Visibility" campaign on your exact-match brand name from day 1 of approval. Rationale: post-June-2025 organic lag means your brand won't rank #1 for days/weeks, so paid bid bridges the gap.
- **Ariel Michaeli (Appfigures, `rp0BY6PJMqc`):** Don't bid on your own app name. Rationale: user searching your brand will tap your ad (paid) before the organic result (free) — you bought a download you'd have gotten free anyway.

**Resolution:** Ariel's logic assumes the app ranks #1 organically for its own brand term. If it does, his advice is right. If it doesn't (post-June-2025 new-app nerf), Lyttle's bridge applies *temporarily*. Brain Coinz at rank #33 for `brain coinz` (`BASELINE_METRICS.md`) is in the Lyttle case — run the Visibility campaign, track daily, switch off the moment organic rank reaches #1 and holds.

### 9. Apple Search Ads Tactical Consensus (both experts agree) ✅

- **NEVER use Search Match.** Verbatim (`qmtDVU9CLv8`): *"Never use search match, please. Search match is awful."* Same verdict from Lyttle — it burns budget on irrelevant keywords.
- **Discovery campaigns are for $100k+ budgets only** (`--DS9GWY8wM`). If total budget is <$10k, Discovery wastes 30%+ on "ideas."
- **ASA keywords should also be in metadata.** Verbatim (`cCwI5BgGhdw`): *"If you don't have a keyword in your metadata and just throw it into Apple ads, Apple will deem it as irrelevant"* — bid prices go up because you're fighting against relevant apps.
- **Separate campaigns by objective.** Visibility / Launch / Experiment — so one keyword draining daily budget can't take brand-protection offline.

### 10. "Is ASO Dead in 2026?" — No, the opposite 🚀

Ariel's direct answer (`xNZCWApikus`): Apple added a second ad placement inside search results (not just at top). This takes *one* visual slot from ASO but signals Apple sees search as high-converting traffic worth monetizing more. Conclusion: *invest more in search, not less*.

### 11. First-30-Days Strategy for New Apps 🆕

Ariel's answer to "For a brand new app, what ASO tactics deliver the fastest visibility boost in the first 30 days?" (`cCwI5BgGhdw`):

1. **Keyword gap analysis.** Use competitor keyword tools to find keywords your 3–5 top competitors are NOT ranking for. Those are your opportunities in the first 30 days.
2. **Don't chase the primary competitive keyword.** Apps that have been pushing at the big keyword for a long time have momentum you can't overcome in 30 days.
3. **"Do less. Always, always, always do less."** From the Hair Hero teardown (`cCwI5BgGhdw`): the common failure is stuffing every possible keyword into the name. Focus on ONE thing. The algorithm splits 100% of its weight across whatever you give it — giving it fewer things concentrates the weight.
