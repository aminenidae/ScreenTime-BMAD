# Weekly Execution Playbook (20 h/week)

Companion to `DISTRIBUTION_STRATEGY.md`. This is the recurring weekly loop once
Month-1 foundations are in place.

---

## Weekly time allocation

| Block | Hours | What |
|---|---|---|
| Ads & analytics | 2 | Apple Ads review, bid/keyword pruning, funnel dashboard check |
| Community | 4 | Reddit / Facebook groups / Quora — ~30–40 min daily |
| Content & SEO | 6 | 1–2 articles or landing-page improvements per week |
| Short-form video | 4 | 2–3 TikTok/Reels/Shorts per week |
| Outreach / PR | 2 | Review sites, parenting newsletters, podcasts — 3–5 pitches/week |
| ASO iteration | 2 | Review responses, PPO screenshot tests, In-App Events, rank tracking |
| **Total** | **20** | |

---

## Block details

### Ads & analytics (Mondays, 2 h)
- Check per-keyword: taps, installs, cost-per-trial. Prune anything with 40+ taps / 0 trials.
- Shift budget toward keywords producing trials, not installs.
- Log weekly numbers in a simple spreadsheet — trendline beats snapshot.

### Community (daily, ~35 min)
- Venues: r/Parenting, r/ScreenTime, r/mildlyinfuriating screen-time threads, local/city
  Facebook parent groups, Quora "limit kids screen time" questions.
- Rules: answer the question fully first; mention the app only when directly relevant and
  always disclosed ("I built an app for exactly this"). Never copy-paste the same reply.
- Save every recurring complaint/question — each is a content idea and an objection to
  handle in screenshots/onboarding.

### Content & SEO (6 h)
- Target long-tail parent queries: "how to make kids earn screen time",
  "screen time reward system", "screen time contract for kids", "apps that limit
  screen time without fights".
- Every article ends with the App Store badge + one screenshot.
- Repurpose: article → 2–3 short videos → community answers.

### Short-form video (4 h)
- Format that fits the product: 15–30 s demo — kid finishes learning app, game unlocks by
  itself, parent never touched the phone. No face required; screen recordings + captions work.
- Post natively to TikTok, Reels, Shorts (same clip, three surfaces).
- Expectation: this is a lottery-ticket channel — most clips do nothing, one hit can
  outperform a year of ads. Cost is already sunk in the 20 h.

### Outreach / PR (2 h)
- Tier 1: SafeWise, Tom's Guide, allaboutcookies.org, TechRadar parental-control roundups.
- Tier 2: parenting newsletters and podcasts (offer founder interview + free family codes).
- Angle: privacy — "the parental control app that can't see your kid's data" (Screen Time
  API, everything stays in the family's iCloud). This is the story competitors can't tell.
- Track pitches in a sheet; follow up once after 7 days, then let it go.

### ASO iteration (2 h)
- Respond to every review (responses are public and improve conversion).
- One Product Page Optimization (screenshot) test running at all times.
- One In-App Event live per month (e.g., "Summer Reading Challenge").
- Weekly rank check on tracked keywords (Astro); watch for post-update re-indexing.

---

## What NOT to spend time on (for now)

- Meta/TikTok/Google paid campaigns (see channel verdict)
- Android port debates — win iOS first
- Influencer outreach beyond micro/newsletter tier — CPMs don't fit the budget
- Building social followings for their own sake — every hour must point at installs or
  learning data
